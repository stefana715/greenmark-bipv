# BIPV × GBRS Meta-Review: Upgraded Master Plan
## Based on 310-Paper Bibliometric Analysis + Gap Verification

**Working Title:**
"How green building rating systems address building-integrated photovoltaics: A systematic review, content-based assessment, and future roadmap"

**Target:** Renewable and Sustainable Energy Reviews (RSER)
**Estimated length:** 10,000–12,000 words + tables/figures

---

## 1. Evidence-Based Gap Statement

From our 310-paper corpus (Scopus, 2010–2025):
- **Only 12 papers (4%)** conduct structured GBRS comparative analysis
- **Only 11 papers** mention a specific GBRS by name (LEED=3, Green Mark=2, ASGB=2)
- **303/310 papers (98%)** discuss PV/BIPV without referencing any GBRS criteria
- **49% are purely technical** (performance/materials) with zero policy/rating connection
- **136 papers** discuss generic PV without distinguishing BIPV application types

**Headline for Introduction:** *"Despite 310 publications at the intersection of BIPV and green buildings, fewer than 4% systematically analyze how rating system criteria shape BIPV adoption — revealing a critical disconnect between technology development and policy instruments."*

The closest existing work — Liu et al. (2021) — compared only 3 systems (LEED, BREEAM, ASGB) through a carbon lens. No study has:
1. Compared 8+ GBRS on BIPV-specific criteria with a structured scoring framework
2. Distinguished how different BIPV types (roof/façade/window/shading) are treated
3. Linked GBRS criteria design to real-world BIPV adoption patterns
4. Provided actionable recommendations for criteria reform

## 2. How to Make This RSER-Level: Five Enhancement Strategies

### Enhancement A: Add a "Policy-Technology Disconnect" Narrative
**The story:** BIPV technology is advancing rapidly (new materials, better efficiency, lower cost), but GBRS criteria haven't kept pace. Most systems still treat BIPV the same as a rooftop panel bolted on after construction. This disconnect means rating systems are inadvertently *slowing* BIPV adoption rather than accelerating it.

**Evidence from our data:**
- 136/310 papers discuss "generic PV" — the literature itself doesn't distinguish BIPV
- Façade BIPV (49 papers) and glazing BIPV (68 papers) are increasingly researched, but GBRS don't have dedicated pathways for them
- The EU EPBD 2024 mandates solar on new buildings from 2027 — GBRS must catch up

**Section in paper:** Discussion subsection "The policy-technology gap"

### Enhancement B: Add "Temporal Evolution Analysis"
**The story:** Track how GBRS have updated their PV/BIPV criteria over time (2010→2025). Show which systems are evolving faster and which are stagnating.

**Method:** For each of the 8 GBRS, compare at least 2 versions:
- LEED v3 (2009) → v4 (2013) → v4.1 (2021)
- BREEAM 2011 → 2014 → 2018
- Green Mark 2015 → 2021
- Green Star v1 → v1.3
- ASGB 2014 → 2019
- BEAM Plus v1 → v2.0
- CASBEE 2014 → 2021
- DGNB 2018 → 2023

**Analysis:** Create a "criteria evolution matrix" showing what changed:
- Did PV points increase?
- Was BIPV explicitly added?
- Were new BIPV types recognized?
- Was a feasibility study requirement introduced?

**This adds massive work volume and shows the trajectory of each system.**

### Enhancement C: Add "Regional Context Mapping"
**The story:** BIPV adoption is deeply influenced by climate, building typology, and energy policy. Link each GBRS to its regional context to explain *why* criteria differ.

**Framework:**
| GBRS | Climate Zone | Dominant Building Type | Solar Resource | Key Policy Driver |
|------|-------------|----------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| LEED | All (global) | Mixed | Varies | US ITC/IRA incentives |
| BREEAM | Cfb (temperate) | Low-mid rise | 1000-1200 kWh/m² | UK Net Zero 2050 |
| Green Mark | Af (tropical) | High-rise | ~1580 kWh/m² | SG Green Plan 2030 |
| Green Star | Mixed (AU) | Mixed | 1400-2200 kWh/m² | AU NCC 2022 |
| ASGB | Mixed (CN) | High-rise dominant | 1000-2000 kWh/m² | CN Dual Carbon goals |
| BEAM Plus | Cwa (subtropical) | Ultra high-rise | ~1400 kWh/m² | HK Climate Action 2050 |
| CASBEE | Cfa/Dfa | Mid-rise | 1000-1500 kWh/m² | JP 2050 Carbon Neutral |
| DGNB | Cfb/Dfb | Low-mid rise | 900-1200 kWh/m² | EU EPBD 2024 |

**Key insight:** High-rise dominant cities (Singapore, Hong Kong) *should* have stronger façade BIPV criteria but often don't. Low-rise contexts (Australia, parts of Europe) can rely on rooftop PV alone but still should encourage BIPV for aesthetic integration.

### Enhancement D: Add "BIPV Technology-Criteria Alignment Analysis"
**The story:** Map emerging BIPV technologies against current GBRS criteria to show where criteria are outdated.

**Matrix:**
| BIPV Technology | TRL | Market Ready? | Recognized by GBRS? | Gap? |
|----------------|-----|---------------|---------------------|------|
| c-Si rooftop tiles | 9 | Yes | All systems | No gap |
| Thin-film façade cladding | 8 | Yes | DGNB only explicitly | **Yes** |
| Semi-transparent PV glazing | 7-8 | Emerging | Almost none | **Yes** |
| Colored/patterned BIPV | 7 | Emerging | None | **Yes** |
| Perovskite tandem BIPV | 5-6 | R&D | None | Future gap |
| PV shading louvers | 8 | Yes | Green Mark partially | **Yes** |
| Flexible PV membrane | 7 | Emerging | None | **Yes** |
| Transparent PV (OPV/DSC) | 5-6 | R&D | None | Future gap |

**Key insight:** GBRS are designed around 2010-era rooftop PV technology. The technology has diversified enormously, but criteria haven't followed.

### Enhancement E: Add "Net-Zero Building Pathway Analysis"
**The story:** With global push toward net-zero buildings, PV/BIPV becomes not just a bonus but a necessity. Analyze how each GBRS defines and supports the net-zero pathway, and what role BIPV plays.

**From our data:** 152/310 papers (49%) focus on Net-Zero/ZEB — this is the dominant theme. But how do GBRS actually define net-zero and mandate PV for it?

**Comparison points:**
- Does the system have a "net-zero" tier? (BREEAM: Outstanding; Green Star: 6-Star; LEED: Zero programs)
- Is PV *required* or just *helpful* for net-zero?
- Can a building achieve net-zero without any on-site PV? (If yes, the system isn't pushing PV hard enough)
- How does BIPV specifically contribute vs. BAPV?

### Section 7: Discussion (2,000 words)
#### 7.1 Synthesis: Why the gap matters
- BIPV market growing at 19.7% CAGR but GBRS criteria are 10 years behind
- Rating systems inadvertently favor rooftop BAPV over integrated BIPV
- High-rise cities (where BIPV is most needed) have weakest façade criteria

#### 7.2 The policy-technology disconnect
- Technology push: new BIPV products available (colored, transparent, flexible)
- Criteria pull: GBRS still use "renewable energy" as a monolithic category
- Result: architects and developers have no specific incentive for BIPV innovation
- Connection to EU EPBD 2024 "solar-ready" mandate

#### 7.3 Regional context implications
- Tropical high-rise (Singapore, Hong Kong): need stronger façade BIPV pathways
- European context (BREEAM, DGNB): EPBD driving rapid evolution
- China: largest construction market but ASGB weakest on BIPV
- Global systems (LEED): flexibility is both strength and weakness

#### 7.4 Lessons from best practices
- Green Mark's mandatory solar feasibility study → should be universal
- DGNB's multi-category BIPV integration → model for holistic assessment
- BREEAM's net-zero carbon pathway → drives maximum PV adoption
- Green Star's innovation credits → rewards novel BIPV applications

#### 7.5 Recommendations for GBRS reform
**For GBRS developers (7 specific recommendations):**
1. Create BIPV-specific sub-credits distinct from generic PV
2. Recognize 4 BIPV application types with differentiated scoring
3. Mandate solar feasibility studies at design stage (BREEAM model)
4. Reference IEC 63092 BIPV standards in assessment criteria
5. Adopt climate-adjusted PV/BIPV targets by region
6. Allow BIPV to earn credits across multiple categories (energy + envelope + materials)
7. Link credits to measured post-occupancy PV performance

**For policymakers (4 recommendations):**
1. Align building codes with GBRS to classify BIPV as building material
2. Create BIPV-specific incentives beyond generic solar subsidies
3. Mandate GBRS alignment with national net-zero building codes
4. Support BIPV demonstration projects as reference cases for GBRS

**For researchers (4 recommendations):**
1. Conduct empirical studies linking GBRS criteria to actual BIPV adoption rates
2. Develop standardized BIPV performance benchmarks for use in GBRS
3. Create urban-scale BIPV potential assessment tools integrated with GBRS
4. Study the economic value of BIPV credits in property valuation

#### 7.6 Limitations
- Content analysis involves judgment (mitigated by rubric transparency)
- NLP-based literature classification has accuracy limits
- GBRS evolve rapidly — snapshot analysis
- Some manuals not freely available

### Section 8: Conclusion (500 words)
- Key contributions (3 numbered)
- Most impactful finding
- Call to action for GBRS reform
- Future research priorities
