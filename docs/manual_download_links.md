## TIER 1: Freely accessible (10 systems)

### 1. LEED v4.1 BD+C

- **Getting Started Guide PDF:** https://www.usgbc.org/resources/leed-v41-bdc-getting-started-guide

- **v4.1 Summary of changes:** https://www.buildinggreen.com/sites/default/files/leed_v4.1_fact_sheet.pdf
- **Key sections:** EAc5 Renewable Energy (5pts), EAc2 Optimize Energy Performance (18pts), IPc1 Integrative Process

### 2. BREEAM NC 2018
- **Online technical manual (free, browsable):** https://www.breeam.com/NC2018/

### 4. BEAM Plus v2.0 (Hong Kong)

### 5. DGNB 2023 (Germany)

## TIER 2: Partially accessible (6 systems)

### 11. Green Star v1.3 (Australia)
- **Credit summaries (free, needs login):** https://new.gbca.org.au/green-star/rating-system/
- **Technical manual requires GBCA membership**
- **Alternative: Use published papers + credit summaries online**
- **Key sections:** Energy category, GHG emission reduction, Innovation Challenge

### 12. ASGB / Three-Star 2019 (China)
- **Standard: GB/T 50378-2019 (Chinese language)**
- **Best English source: Liu et al. (2021) paper + Liu et al. (2022) paper**
- **Partial English summary in:** https://www.mdpi.com/2075-5309/11/10/469
- **Key sections:** Section 8.2.9 Renewable Energy (~10/400 points)

### 13. HQE v3 (France)

- **RE2020 regulation context:** https://www.ecologie.gouv.fr/politiques-publiques/reglementation-environnementale-re2020
- **Key info:** Aligned with French RE2020 which requires lifecycle carbon + renewable contribution

### 16. GSAS/QSAS v2.1 (Qatar)

## Recommended reading order (most impactful first)
1. Green Mark 2021 — your strongest BIPV case, Singapore context
2. LEED v4.1 — most widely used globally
3. BREEAM NC 2018 — European leader, mandatory LZC study
4. DGNB 2023 — best BIPV-envelope integration
5. Living Building Challenge — most ambitious (net-positive)
6. Green Star — net-zero pathway
7. Passive House — energy-focused, Plus/Premium RE requirements
8. BEAM Plus — dense urban/high-rise context
9. Estidama Pearl — hot climate context
10. ASGB — largest market, use Liu et al. papers
